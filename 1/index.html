Part 1: Selfie

This project explores the varying perspective effects achieved in portrait photography at different distances and focal lengths. I conducted two sets of shots using my own camera: one featuring close-up wide-angle selfies and another with distant zoom selfies, comparing their differences.

Take me as an example...

Wide-angle selfies at different distances

From left to right: 14mm, 24mm, 48mm, 77 mm

Think about why this is...

The wide-angle selfies taken at close distances (e.g., 14mm and 24mm) produce significant perspective distortion. This exaggerates facial features like the nose, making them appear disproportionately large and out of proportion.

As the focal length increases (e.g., 48mm and 77mm), this distortion is significantly reduced, resulting in a more natural and flattering representation of the face.

The 77mm shot, in particular, closely resembles how we perceive faces in real life, with balanced proportions and minimal distortion.
